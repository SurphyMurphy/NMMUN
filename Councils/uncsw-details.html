United Nations Commission On The Status of Women


Topic 1: Regulation of child marriage laws

Prevalence of child marriage has decreased worldwide, from one in four girls married a decade ago to approximately one in five today, however, the practice remains widespread. Before the COVID-19 pandemic, more than 100 million girls were expected to marry before their eighteenth birthday. Now, up to 10 million more girls will be at risk of becoming child brides as a result of the pandemic. Child marriage is caused due to entrenched gender inequality, while child marriage in boys is just 1/6th among girls. Under age, marriage robs a girl of her basic ability to dream, have an education, and access many resources. Women not only endure physical abuse from child marriage but it also harms their mental well being leaving them often dependent on their host families. Member nations of this council are requested to find possible solutions on how to stop this age-old practice and must work together to strengthen the laws of their respective countries.



Topic 2: Gender-Based Violence in conflict and post-conflict zones

Women and girls suffer disproportionately from violent conflict which can result in higher levels of gender-based violence against women and girls, including arbitrary killings, torture, violence, and forced marriage. A Significant Increase in Gender Based Violence has also been observed in post-conflict societies, this is mainly due to the lack of enforcement of the law, the breakdown of social and family structures, and the “normalization” of gender-based violence. This is accompanied by the lack of delivery of essential services and healthcare and obstacles in accessing education. The delegates of the UNCSW must work together to eliminate or alleviate these side effects of conflicts.
