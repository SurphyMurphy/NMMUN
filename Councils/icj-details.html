Topic: The legality of the capture and sentencing of Khulbushan Sudhir Jadhav (India vs Pakistan)

In April 2017, Kulbhushan Sudhir Jadhav, an Indian national, was sentenced to death by a Pakistani military court for engaging in acts of terrorism and espionage in Pakistan. The following month India filed application proceedings against Pakistan concerning the alleged violations of Article 36 of the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations (VCCR). The claim was made by India that they failed to be informed of the arrest and detainment of one of their citizens. It was further insisted that the rights under article 36 of the VCCR were not stated to Mr. Jadhav and that communication between India's consular officers had been illegally revoked. Pakistan claims that Article 36 rights do not apply to activities concerned with “espionage”. Pakistan also pushes that the special agreement on consular relations (2008 bilateral agreement) allows both parties involved to judge individual cases' “merits”. Has Pakistan breached its obligations? Do the obligations imposed by the Vienna Convention and Consular Relations Article 36 apply to all VCCR state party nationals in another's territory?
