Topic 1: Strengthening the capacity of integrating Ukrainian refugees into resettlement countries

The Russian Federation invaded Ukraine on February 24th 2022. With a stated goal of demilitarizing the Ukrainian Government. The 2022 invasion of Ukraine has resulted in a massive refugee crisis. The 41 million people who inhabit Ukraine make it the 7th most populous country in Europe and One month since its invasion almost 3.7 million people have fled the country, this is over half the refugees produced by the Syrian civil war in its 10 year duration. March 4th the European Union unanimously approved temporary protection for those fleeing the war in Ukraine for one year, nearly an additional 4 million have escaped to European countries. Many countries are also accepting Ukrainian refugees,currently there are 34 countries serving as resettlement countries and accepting them and other national refugees. State capacity for resettlement is declining while the demand for resettlement places is on the rise, Despite their advanced economies and scales of resources, resettlement states face the challenge of allocating finite resources to various priorities with domestic politics in mind. When some citizens resettlement states express exclusionary attitudes toward culturally-different groups, states must come together to formulate short-term and long term policies of accepting Ukrainian refugees and integrating them into society and the work force and potentially thinking about reintegrating them back to Ukraine



Topic 2: Displacement caused by climate emergency

The impacts of our changing climate are being felt worldwide, but countries already struggling with conflict, poverty and high levels of displacement are dealing with some of the most severe effects. From Afghanistan to Central America, droughts, flooding and other extreme weather events are hitting those least equipped to recover and adapt. Disasters linked to climate change may worsen poverty, food insecurity and access to natural resources in ways that can stoke instability and violence. Many of the countries most exposed to the impacts of climate change are already host to large numbers of refugees and internally displaced people.The delegates of the UNHCR must discuss both preventive solutions to the issue and means to assist those already displaced.
